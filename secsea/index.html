Passive recon strategy

Identify vehicle version and specification (Be sure all things matches) :

  • Model codename
  • Engine codename (may have many)
  • ECU version

Passive recon strategy (cont.)

  • Search for dedicated forums, look for the exact model version or for a very similar version.
  • Look at CAN protocol used by ECU and try to get the datasheet or at least the Programation Suite.
  • Begin to identify electronic component, you can buy ECUs of interrest on eBay.
  • Look for available ressources on the internet (press, binary dump, fails, datasheets)

    I came across an interesting ECU so I thought I'd share. I hope this helps someone. It is a genuine Group N racing ECU for the vacuum advance PE7 ECU. The guy who showed me it is an ex Honda mechanic (of 30 something years), and used to race in the Group N class. It came out of a genuine Honda box with a PE7 sticker on the side. It had a raised ECU top cover (pressed from the factory) to accomodate the ZIF sockets. Naturally, curiosity got the better of me and I soon had it in pieces. I took pics of the disassembly of the ECU. If anyone feels like sifting through the 2 x 8kb bin files attached, go for it. From what I understand, the rev-limit has been set to 9000rpm, if that helps. The bin files have been labelled, as they were placed in the sockets. It seems weird that the 128kb stock ECU EPROM has been divided into 2 x 64kb EPROMs. Maybe that helps explain why disassembling the code has been so trivial. I have thoroughly tested the pinouts, and have come up with the attached schematic in Eagle format. Hope this helps someone . . . .
